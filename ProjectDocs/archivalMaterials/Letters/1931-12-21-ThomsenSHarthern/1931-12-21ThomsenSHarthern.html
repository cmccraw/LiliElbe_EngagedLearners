Style Guide

  • The following is a guide to our encoding style:
  • People and Places
  • Diary Entry
  • Text added in another hand: details can be seen on mouse-over.
  • Text removed: details can be seen on mouse-over.
  • The original text has idiosyncratic spelling and the regularized text can be seen on mouse-over OR missing punctuation is restored in the text and the error can be seen on mouse-over.
  • The @ symbol indicates a comment by either a project editor or translator. The comment can be seen on mouse-over of the @ symbol and the team member can be identified in the “Document Metadata”.
  • Page numbers appear in the left-hand margin when the paragraph begins on a new page; when a paragraph spans two pages, page numbers appear in brackets inline with the text.
  • Clicking on the page numbers, in the "Transcription" column, reveals the digital facsimile of the original page.

Document Metadata

Team member project IDs can be seen on mouse-over of their name. These IDs are used throughout the archival texts— linking editor and translator notes to the team member responsible for the note.
